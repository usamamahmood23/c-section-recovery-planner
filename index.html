Understanding your recovery
Red flags — contact your doctor
Call your provider immediately if you experience:
  • Fever above 38°C / 100.4°F
  • Increasing redness, swelling, or warmth around incision
  • Foul-smelling discharge from incision
  • Heavy bleeding (soaking more than 1 pad per hour)
  • Severe abdominal pain not relieved by medication
  • Signs of blood clot: calf pain, swelling, warmth in leg
  • Feeling unable to cope, persistent low mood
Nutrition for recovery

Protein: Aim for 1.2–1.5g per kg of body weight to support tissue repair.

Key nutrients: Vitamin C (wound healing), Iron (blood loss recovery), Calcium, Omega-3s.

Hydration: 2–3L water per day, more if breastfeeding.

Breastfeeding & exercise

It is safe to exercise while breastfeeding. A few tips:

  • Feed or pump just before exercise for comfort
  • Stay well hydrated — milk is mostly water
  • Wear a supportive sports bra (not too compressive)
  • Moderate exercise does not affect milk supply or taste
